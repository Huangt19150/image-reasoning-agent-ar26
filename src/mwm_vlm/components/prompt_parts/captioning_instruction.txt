**Instruction for Image Captioning of Protein Crystallization Droplets**

You are given a microscopy image of a protein crystallization droplet. Your task is to produce a detailed and accurate **caption** that reflects all relevant observable features needed to interpret the crystallization result. The caption should help a protein crystallographer assess the outcome and determine next steps for optimization or validation.

**Your caption must identify and describe the following, where applicable:**

1. **Overall Clarity of the Drop**

   * Indicate if the drop is completely clear (Score = 0), contains non-protein particles (Score = 1), or shows visible precipitation or crystallization.

2. **Precipitate Characteristics**

   * Note whether precipitate is **amorphous** (no birefringence, no sharp edges), **gelatinous** (transparent or white, Score = 4), or **birefringent** (bright or glowing under polarized light).
   * Mention precipitate **distribution** (uniform, patchy, peripheral, etc.) and **color** (dark = likely denatured protein; light/transparent = more promising).

3. **Signs of Phase Separation or Skin Formation**

   * Describe presence of **oily films**, **droplet-in-droplet appearance**, or surface **films** (indicating phase separation or denaturation).

4. **Crystal Presence and Morphology**

   * If crystals are visible, describe:

     * **Dimensions**: one-dimensional (needles), two-dimensional (plates), or three-dimensional (blocks).
     * **Size estimate** (e.g., 0.3 x 0.1 mm).
     * **Quantity**: single, clusters, or showers.
     * **Birefringence** under polarized light.
     * **Edge definition** and **symmetry**.
     * Use terms like “microcrystals,” “spherulites,” or “needle bundles” if appropriate.

5. **Artifacts or Contaminants**

   * Mention visible non-protein artifacts: glass shards, dust, fibers, or bacterial growth.

6. **Illumination or Imaging Notes**

   * If polarized light was used, describe whether **birefringence** is present and how distinct it is.
   * Indicate if **dark-field** illumination revealed hidden structures such as gelatinous precipitate.

**Example Caption Template:**

> “The drop appears mostly clear with faint gelatinous precipitate visible under dark-field illumination (Score = 4). No birefringence is detected. A few microcrystals (approx. 10–20 µm) are present near the edge, showing faint birefringence (Score = 6). No signs of phase separation or surface skin. No visible contaminants.”
